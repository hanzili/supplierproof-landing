Federal Plastics Registry / plastic reporting

Plastic reporting is becoming a data-exchange problem across facilities, vendors, franchisees, and recyclers.

We are researching how companies prepare for plastic reporting: resin categories, products placed on market, waste generated, facility streams, service-provider data, and internal handoffs between packaging, EHS, ops, procurement, and finance.

15 min workflow callWe are looking for operators to sanity-check this.

What we think is happening

  1. Company determines whether it reports as manufacturer/importer, waste generator, service provider, or another role.
  2. Teams identify resin/product categories and reporting boundaries.
  3. Data must be pulled from facilities, packaging systems, purchase/order data, waste vendors, recyclers, or franchise locations.
  4. Missing or estimated data needs caveats and follow-up.
  5. Final submission needs an audit trail for how quantities were derived.

Agent/service hypothesis

A plastic reporting readiness desk that converts reporting requirements into data requests, tracks facility/vendor responses, flags missing evidence, and prepares a submission support packet.

Where admin breaks

Role confusion

Different obligations for manufacturers/importers, waste generators, service providers, and franchise structures.

Data scattered by site

Facilities, procurement, packaging, and waste vendors all hold part of the answer.

Classification work

Resin/product categories and residential vs ICI streams need consistent mapping.

New regulation uncertainty

Teams may not know what is reasonable, what can be estimated, or who must report up.

Questions we want to ask operators

  • Who owns plastic reporting inside your company?
  • Which data is already tracked and which requires vendor/facility follow-up?
  • Are you using order data, waste manifests, recycler reports, or estimates?
  • Where do you expect the biggest missing-data issue?
  • Can we see a redacted internal tracker or reporting checklist?
