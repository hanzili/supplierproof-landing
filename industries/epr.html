EPR / producer responsibility reporting

Producer reporting gets stuck in forms, emails, signatures, and missing sales data.

We are researching how EPR teams and producer responsibility organizations chase complete member reports: product type, volume, weight, province/region, back-reporting, signatures, and fee-impacting corrections.

15 min workflow callRedacted forms or exports welcome.

What we think is happening

  1. Producer/member receives onboarding or reporting request.
  2. They half-complete a PDF, portal form, or spreadsheet.
  3. Ops/member-relations team finds missing signatures, product fields, province splits, or weights.
  4. Staff chase by email, phone, translation, and reminders until report is complete.
  5. Finance uses reported volumes/sales to calculate fees or back-reporting.

Agent/service hypothesis

A reporting desk that checks submissions for completeness, drafts the exact follow-up, tracks member status, translates plain-language requests, and returns a review-ready exception list before finance/audit.

Where admin breaks

Incomplete submissions

Missing pages, signatures, SKUs, volume, weights, region/province splits.

Touchpoint overload

Some members require dozens of email interactions before becoming complete.

Fee/revenue risk

Bad reporting means wrong fees, back-charges, audit exceptions, or compliance exposure.

Language + low-tech users

Chinese suppliers, Québec French nuance, farmers printing/scanning PDFs.

Questions we want to ask operators

  • Which fields are most often missing or wrong?
  • How do you track incomplete producers/members today?
  • Where does member-relations hand off to data/finance?
  • What redacted form, report export, or exception list could we study?
  • What would make a one-request pilot useful in one week?
