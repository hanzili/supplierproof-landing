Mining voluntary assurance

Mining teams reuse the same evidence across frameworks, but the mapping still lives in spreadsheets.

We are researching how sustainability, EHS, and assurance teams prepare support for voluntary mining standards and external verification: site evidence, policies, source data, owner sign-offs, and framework overlap.

15 min workflow callRedacted protocol/checklist welcome.

What we think is happening

  1. Corporate team selects voluntary standards/protocols and assurance scope.
  2. Sites gather evidence across tailings, water, safety, community, Indigenous relations, climate, biodiversity, and governance.
  3. Evidence is stored across SharePoint, email, spreadsheets, procedures, invoices, logs, and site folders.
  4. Assurance asks for source support, not just management explanations.
  5. The same evidence may support several frameworks, but reuse is manual.

Agent/service hypothesis

A mining evidence desk that maps proof once to multiple frameworks, flags assurance gaps, routes site-owner follow-ups, and generates a support packet for human review.

Where admin breaks

Framework overlap

TSM, ICMM-aligned standards, Copper Mark/IRMA-like asks may require similar proof in different formats.

Site-level ownership

Corporate needs evidence from many locations and owners, each with different document habits.

Assurance evidence quality

Reasonable assurance requires source data, logs, invoices, and support trails.

Excel-heavy control

Even mature industries still exchange evidence and status in spreadsheets.

Questions we want to ask operators

  • Which standards/protocols create duplicate evidence work?
  • What evidence is hardest to collect from sites?
  • How do you track owner, source, expiry, and assurance status?
  • Where does external verification usually get stuck?
  • Can we review a redacted protocol/evidence checklist?
