Food & packaging suppliers

Customer sustainability requests turn into internal scavenger hunts.

Retailers and strategic customers ask for CDP, Walmart Gigaton, EcoVadis, packaging claims, food safety certificates, palm/fiber sourcing proof, and facility data. The pain is usually not one calculation — it is finding evidence, chasing owners, and preparing a safe response.

What we think is happening

  1. Customer sends a questionnaire, portal request, or evidence checklist.
  2. Sustainability/procurement/QA maps questions to bills, certificates, supplier declarations, specs, and policies.
  3. Missing data sits with finance, procurement, quality, facility ops, or upstream suppliers.
  4. Team drafts answers with caveats, attachment lists, and approval notes.
  5. The same evidence gets reused for the next customer, but usually not cleanly.

Agent/service hypothesis

An embedded request desk that turns one customer ask into owner-level tasks, evidence mapping, supplier/internal follow-ups, and an approval-ready response package.

Common evidence objects

CDP / emissions

Reporting year, facility bills, category assumptions, Scope 1/2/3 data.

Walmart Gigaton / retailer asks

Packaging, waste, energy, agriculture, commodity, and reduction claims.

EcoVadis / THESIS-like assessments

Policies, certificates, corrective actions, and proof attachments.

Packaging + sourcing proof

Recycled content, recyclability, RSPO/FSC, supplier declarations, spec sheets.

Questions we want to ask operators

  • Which customer request creates the most admin?
  • How many people/departments touch one response?
  • What proof gets reused, and what gets recreated every time?
  • Would you pay for help finishing one live request?
  • Can we review a redacted customer request or old response packet?
